Human exploration of Mars presents enormous engineering challenges,
including radiation exposure, the long transit time between planets, and
the difficulty of landing heavy payloads safely on the Martian surface.
Spacecraft designers must account for months of isolation, limited
resupply options, and the need to produce oxygen, water, and fuel using
local resources once astronauts arrive. Robotic precursor missions have
already mapped potential landing sites and tested technologies that a
future crewed mission would depend on.
