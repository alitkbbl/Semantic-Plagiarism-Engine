Renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power are
becoming increasingly vital as the world tries to reduce its reliance on
fossil fuels. Solar panels convert sunlight directly into electricity,
while wind turbines capture the kinetic energy of moving air. Unlike coal
or natural gas, these sources emit little to no greenhouse gas during
operation, making them a key part of any strategy to fight climate
change. Several governments now offer tax incentives to encourage
households and businesses to invest in renewable infrastructure, and
some cities have set ambitious targets for full adoption by 2035.
