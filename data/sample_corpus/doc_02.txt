As the planet moves away from fossil fuels, renewable sources of power
like solar, wind, and hydroelectric energy have grown steadily more
important. Photovoltaic panels turn sunlight straight into electrical
power, and wind turbines harness the kinetic motion of the air. Compared
with coal or natural gas, these alternatives release almost no greenhouse
gases while running, which makes them essential to any serious plan for
fighting climate change. Governments around the world increasingly offer
tax breaks to push homeowners and companies toward renewable
infrastructure investment.
