Sending humans to Mars involves huge engineering obstacles, among them
radiation exposure, the lengthy journey between the two planets, and the
challenge of safely landing heavy equipment on the Martian surface.
Spacecraft engineers have to plan for months of isolation, scarce
resupply opportunities, and the need to generate oxygen, water, and fuel
from resources found on site once astronauts touch down. Earlier robotic
missions have already surveyed candidate landing sites and proven out
technology a future human mission would rely on.
