Renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power are
becoming increasingly important as the world seeks to reduce its reliance
on fossil fuels. Solar panels convert sunlight directly into electricity,
while wind turbines capture the kinetic energy of moving air. Unlike coal
or natural gas, these sources produce little to no greenhouse gas
emissions during operation, making them a critical part of any strategy
to combat climate change. Many governments now offer tax incentives to
encourage households and businesses to invest in renewable infrastructure.
